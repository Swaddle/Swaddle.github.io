Numerical investigation of geodesics for optimal quantum circuits

  • A manifold is a space that looks locally Euclidean.
First we define the control hamiltonian; \[ \mathcal{K}(t) = \sum_{i} k_i(t) \;\sigma_i \] Where the \( \sigma \) are generalised pauli matrices. They are the "directions" we can move in the manifold. If we re-write out variational problem, we get two equations we want to solve. First one for the control hamiltonian; \[ \mathcal{K}'(t) = \mathcal{C}^{-1} [ \mathcal{C} \mathcal{K}(t) ,\mathcal{K}(t) ] \] And one for the geodesic itself; \[ \mathcal{X}'(t) = \mathcal{K}(t) \mathcal{X}(t) \]
The problem is these equations are in a very high dimensional manifold. \(\mathcal{X}(t)\) and \(\mathcal{K}(t)\) are curves in the space of special unitary matrices \(\text{SU}(2^n)\). Thus we have \(2^n \times 2^n\) equations we need to solve.
